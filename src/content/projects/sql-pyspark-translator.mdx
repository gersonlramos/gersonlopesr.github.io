## Problem

Teams migrating workloads from SQL-based warehouses to Spark often need to hand-translate existing queries into PySpark code. For anything beyond a trivial `SELECT`, that manual translation — juggling joins, aliases, aggregates, and `GROUP BY` clauses correctly — is slow and error-prone, and mistakes are easy to miss until a job produces the wrong numbers downstream.

## Approach

A custom lexer/parser tokenizes the incoming SQL query, builds an intermediate representation (a query AST) that captures the query's structure independent of its original syntax, and then generates equivalent PySpark DataFrame code from that representation (see diagram above). The translator specifically handles joins, column aliases, aggregate functions, and `GROUP BY` clauses — the constructs that make manual translation error-prone in the first place.
